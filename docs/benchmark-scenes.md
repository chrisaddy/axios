# Axios Benchmark Scenes

These benchmark scenes define the emotional and visual target for the first slice of Axios. If these scenes work, the rest of the art direction has a reliable center of gravity.

## Benchmark Scene 1: Church Courtyard at Dusk

### Purpose
This is the core mood benchmark for sacred anticipation. It should establish Axios as reverent, luminous, and grounded in place.

### Scene description
The player enters a stone church courtyard in the late evening. The sky is dimming toward amber and violet. A few lamps have already been lit beneath the portico. Clergy and laypeople pass through quietly. A deaconess speaks with a widow near a basin. Candlelight glows faintly through the church doorway while the first hints of chant or preparation emerge from within.

### Emotional goal
- reverence
- invitation
- peace touched by expectation
- a sense that something holy is happening nearby, but not theatrically announced

### Visual ingredients
- limewashed walls catching the last warm light
- stone paving worn by many feet
- one tree or vertical plant form softening the courtyard geometry
- oil lamps under archways
- dark doorway with warm interior glow
- modest cloth banners or curtains moving slightly in evening air
- figures arranged with dignity and quiet activity

### Color target
- warm stone
- dusk violet
- honey lamp glow
- muted red and blue garments as focal accents
- gold used only in glimpses inside the doorway or on select objects

### Lighting target
- long soft shadows
- low-angle sunlight fading at scene edge
- lamp glow creating pockets of intimacy
- clear contrast between ordinary exterior and sacred interior threshold

### Gameplay function
- social hub
- quest handoff / return point
- reflection and progression space
- place where earlier quest consequences visibly affect NPC presence and tone

### Production notes
If only one scene gets special treatment early, it should be this one.

## Benchmark Scene 2: Market Street at Midday

### Purpose
This is the benchmark for lived urban density and historical readability. It proves Constantinople feels inhabited rather than symbolic only.

### Scene description
A sunlit market street lined with awnings, baskets, amphorae, cloth merchants, food sellers, laborers, and passersby. The street bends slightly, creating curiosity about what lies beyond. A dispute is taking place near a stall, children dart through a side passage, and a porter unloads goods while a catechumen contact tries to get the player's attention.

### Emotional goal
- curiosity
- bustle
- social complexity
- liveliness without comedic chaos

### Visual ingredients
- striped or dyed awnings
- crates, jars, woven baskets, cloth bolts
- layered signage or symbolic shop markers
- workers, merchants, household buyers, and poor petitioners sharing the same space
- visible heat and brightness
- narrow side lanes branching into residential space

### Color target
- limestone and plaster neutrals
- terracotta
- faded cloth reds and greens
- sea-blue accents where appropriate
- sun-bleached surfaces with stronger garment accents

### Lighting target
- bright midday sun
- short but legible shadows
- reflected light off pale surfaces
- a sense of warmth and exposure

### Gameplay function
- investigation and dialogue-rich zone
- place for quests involving trade, fairness, rumor, and local reputation
- good test bed for navigation clarity and NPC staging

### Production notes
This scene proves the game can carry active quest play without losing the art style.

## Benchmark Scene 3: Widow's House Interior by Lamp Light

### Purpose
This is the benchmark for intimacy, dignity, and human-scale drama. It proves Axios can handle tenderness and poverty without sentimentality or uglification.

### Scene description
A modest domestic interior in the evening. The room is small but cared for. A lamp burns low. A shelf holds a few vessels, cloth, and perhaps one devotional object or practical household token. The widow sits or stands near a work area, speaking softly. The player's presence should feel personal and consequential. Outside, the city is still audible but muted.

### Emotional goal
- tenderness
- seriousness
- trust
- moral clarity emerging from lived hardship

### Visual ingredients
- rough plaster walls
- simple textiles
- one wooden stool or table
- small ceramic vessels
- folded cloth or mending materials
- localized lamp glow
- deep shadow in room edges
- a tiny hint of the outside city through a window or curtain gap

### Color target
- warm umber and candle amber
- muted cloth reds or blues
- soft plaster neutrals
- dark brown shadow masses
- minimal accent gold, if any

### Lighting target
- mostly lamp-lit
- gentle falloff into shadow
- face and hands strongly composed by light
- quiet reflective tone

### Gameplay function
- dialogue-heavy moral quest scene
- relationship and consequence space
- ideal location for one of the most emotionally important moments in the MVP

### Production notes
This scene sets the standard for how Axios portrays domestic and charitable life.

## Optional Future Benchmark Scene 4: Feast Procession or Vigil

### Purpose
This is a stretch benchmark for later in MVP production. It demonstrates communal joy, ritual scale, and consequence payoff.

### Scene description
A district space transformed by feast preparation: lamps, gathered neighbors, clergy movement, chant, fabrics, and a richer sense of public sacred time.

### Emotional goal
- communal beauty
- culmination
- relief and gratitude
- sacred festivity without carnival excess

### Use later for
- trailer moments
- vertical-slice finale polish
- verifying that player choices visually alter community response

## Benchmark evaluation checklist

A benchmark scene succeeds if:
- it is instantly recognizable as late antique Mediterranean Christian urban space
- it feels explorable and readable as a game level
- it communicates a distinct emotional register through light, composition, and color
- it avoids generic fantasy aesthetics
- it makes the player want to dwell in the world, not just pass through it
